 Due to current events: integration of third-party providers into websites and apps
 

Due to a large number of inquiries, complaints and control suggestions, the
State Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information of the Free Hanseatic City of Bremen
the following:
 
With regard to the legal assessment of the integration of third-party providers on websites and apps
the supervisory authorities of the federal and state governments have agreed in the orientation guide of the
supervisory authorities for providers of telemedia1 on a common understanding of the law
agreed.
 
The orientation guide applies in principle to all data processing by products and services
Services that website and app operators use in particular for website analysis
can operate.
 
In this context, it is again pointed out that former publications,
which, taking into account the legal situation, were communicated before May 25, 2018, such as
For example, the "Notes from the HmbBfDI on the use of Google Analytics", also because
that the processing processes of Google Analytics were continuously adjusted
and are no longer represented by any federal or state supervisory authority.
 
Website and app operators are according to Article 5 paragraph 1 and 2 of the General Data Protection Regulation
(GDPR) obliges to ensure the legality of the processing of personal data
Evidence of third-party involvement.
 
If the Terms of Use or
Guidelines it is stated that processing of personal data is also to own
purposes of the provider takes place, the use of such a product (subject to a
specific assessment on a case-by-case basis) based on the criteria set out in the Guidance
have been established, the leeway that the legal basis of Article 6 paragraph 1
Letter f GDPR offers, span. Therefore, such products and services are only available
Basis of an effective consent according to Article 6 paragraph 1 letter a DSGVO
data protection compliant.
