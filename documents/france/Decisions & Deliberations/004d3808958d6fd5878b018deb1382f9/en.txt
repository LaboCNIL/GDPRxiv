Deliberation 2019-021 of February 28, 2019National Commission for Computing and LibertiesNature of the deliberation: Members and agentsLegal status: In force Date of publication on Légifrance: Tuesday March 12, 2019NOR: CNIL1906966XDeliberation No. 2019-021 of February 28, 2019 on delegation of powers of the National Commission for Computing and Liberties to its president and its deputy vice-presidentThe National Commission for Computing and Liberties,
Having regard to Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of individuals with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data, and repealing Directive 95/46/EC;
Considering the modified law n° 78-17 of January 6, 1978 relating to data processing, files and freedoms, in particular its article 15;
Considering the decree n° 2005-1309 of October 20, 2005 modified taken for the application of the law n° 78-17 of January 6, 1978 relating to data processing, files and freedoms, in particular its article 34;
Having regard to the decree of January 31, 2019 appointing the President of the National Commission for Computing and Liberties - Ms. DENIS (Marie-Laure);
Having regard to deliberation no. 2019-014 of February 21, 2019 on the election of the deputy vice-president of the National Commission for Computing and Liberties;
After having heard Mrs. Marie-Laure DENIS, President, in her report, and Mrs. Nacima BELKACEM, Government Commissioner, in her observations,
Decides: Article 1 Learn more about this article...
I. - Pursuant to article 15 of the aforementioned law of January 6, 1978, delegation is granted to the president of the National Commission for Data Processing and Liberties and to the deputy vice-president for the purpose of exercise the powers mentioned in the following articles of the same law:
- to e and f of 2° of article 11;
- in c of 2° of article 11;
- at d of 4° of article 11;
- Articles 41, 42 and 70-22;
- Articles 54, 62 and 64.
II. - Pursuant to article 15 of the law of January 6, 1978 referred to above, delegation is also given to the president of the National Commission for Computing and Liberties and to the deputy vice-president for the purpose of exercising the allocations mentioned in the following provisions of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of April 27, 2016 referred to above:
- in 4 of Article 34, only for decisions acknowledging compliance with the conditions mentioned in 3 of the same Article 34;
- to a and h of 3 of article 58.Article 2 Find out more about this article...
However, the Commission remains competent to adopt, on the initiative of the president or the deputy vice-president, those of the reference systems, standard regulations and reference methodologies mentioned in articles 54 and 62 of the law of January 6, 1978 referred to above, as well as those of the authorizations mentioned in articles 54 and 64 of the same law, which present particular difficulties or complexity.
It also remains competent to adopt, on the initiative of the President or the Deputy Vice-President, those of the decisions, opinions and authorizations mentioned in the aforementioned provisions of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of April 27, 2016 referred to above which present difficulties. or particular complexity.Article 3
Deliberation no. 2014-073 of February 4, 2014 delegating the powers of the National Commission for Computing and Liberties to its president and its deputy vice-president is repealed. Article 4
The President of the Commission is responsible for the execution of this deliberation, which will be published in the Official Journal of the French Republic.
