SAN-2021-004 of April 30, 2021 relating injunction issued against GOOGLE LLC and GOOGLE IRELAND LIMITED by deliberation n°2020-012 of December 7, 2020 and Philippe-Pierre CABOURDIN, vice-president, and Ms Anne DEBET and Christine MAUGÜE, members; Having regard to regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 relating to the protection of personal data and to the free circulation of this data; Having regard to law no. 78-17 of 6 January 1978 as amended relating to data processing, files and freedoms, in particular its articles 20 and following; Having regard to decree no. 2019-536 of 29 May i 2019 caught for law enforcement
no. 78-17 of January 6, 1978 as amended relating to data processing, files and freedoms; Having regard to deliberation no. 2013-175 of July 4, 2013 adopting the internal regulations of the National Commission for Data Processing and Freedoms; deliberation no. 2020-012 of December 7, 2020 pronouncing a sanction against the companies GOOGLE LLC and GOOGLE IRELAND LIMITED; Having regard to the elements transmitted by the company GOOGLE IRELAND LIMITED on December 18, 2020 and March 30, 2021; Having regard to the other documents of the file; After deliberation, adopted the following decision: I. Facts and procedure Deliberation no. 2020-012 of December 7, 2020 ordered GOOGLE LLC and GOOGLE IRELAND LIMITED to: bring the processing into compliance with the obligations Article 82 of the Data Protection Act and, in particular, to inform the persons concerned beforehand and in a clear and complete manner, for example on the information banner present on the home page of the google.fr site: - the purposes of all cookies subject to consent, - the means at their disposal to refuse them. This injunction was accompanied by a penalty of 100,000 euros per day of delay at the end of a period three months following the notification of the deliberation, the supporting documents of compliance must be sent to the restricted training within this period. changes that it planned to deploy on the google.fr website by the end of February 2021 and to which the CNIL services responded on February 17, 2021. On March 30, 2021, within the deadline set by the deliberation , the company GOOGLE IRELAND LIMITED sent the Restricted Committee information in order to justify its compliance. II. Reasons for the decision 21 that people visiting the google.fr site are now clearly and fully informed of all the purposes of cookies subject to consent and the means available to them to refuse them, through the information banner displayed on their arrival on the site. Consequently, the Restricted Committee considers that the companies GOOGLE LLC and GOOGLE IRELAND LIMITED have complied with the injunction within the time limit. This decision will be made public as had been the deliberation no. 2020-012 of December 7, 2020.FOR THESE REASONSThe restricted committee of the CNIL, after deliberation, decides:- that there is no need to liquidate the penalty payment;- to make public, on the website of the CNIL and on the Légifrance site, its deliberation, which will no longer identify the companies by name as of December 11, 2022.
