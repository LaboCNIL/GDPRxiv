July 2019 plenary of the European Data Protection Board

On July 9-10, 2019, during the Plenary of the European Committee for Data Protection, a body with legal personality of the European Union, established under art. 68 of the General Data Protection Regulation, the following documents were mainly adopted:
- Guide no. 3/2019 on data processing by means of video surveillance - version for public consultation
The Committee has adopted this guide to support operators and authorized persons using video surveillance, which mainly address issues of legality of processing, disclosure of images to third parties, confidentiality and security of processing.
The guide contains many edifying examples and is in public consultation until September 6, 2019, when proposals can be submitted to us.

- Opinion no. 8/2019 on the competence of the supervisory authority in case of change of circumstances regarding the main / sole headquarters of the operator;
In the event of a change in the circumstances regarding the principal / sole place of business of the operator, the power to monitor and control may revert to another national supervisory authority as long as the original competent supervisory authority has not taken a final decision. The cooperation procedure provided in art. 60 of the General Data Protection Regulation will continue to apply and the new authority will cooperate with the original authority and the other authorities involved.

- Opinion no. 9/2019 on the draft Data Protection Authority of Austria on accreditation criteria for codes of conduct monitoring bodies

- Opinion no. 14/2019 on the draft of the Data Protection Authority of Denmark regarding the standard contractual clauses for operators pursuant to art. 28 para. 8 of the General Data Protection Regulation
