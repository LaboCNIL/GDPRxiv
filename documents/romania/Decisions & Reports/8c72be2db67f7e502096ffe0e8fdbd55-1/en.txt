Top European Fines

According to the data of a recently published survey, Romania ranks third in the European Union in terms of fines applied by national supervisory authorities, starting from 2018 until now.
Thus, the National Supervisory Authority for the Processing of Personal Data applied, starting from May 25, 2018, a number of 68 sanctions with a fine of 721,000 euros, which places Romania after Spain and Italy.
In the same ranking, Hungary ranks fourth with a number of 45 fine sanctions applied, and Norway ranks fifth with 40 sanctions.
According to the data from the survey, in the period 2018-2021, in the European Union fines were applied in the total amount of:

 436,000 euros for 2018
 72 million euros for 2019
 171 million euros for 2020
 1 billion euros for 2021.
