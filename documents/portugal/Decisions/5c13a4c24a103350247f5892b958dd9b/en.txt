NATIONAL DATA PROTECTION COMMISSION
REGISTRATION No. 7/2018
VIHUME, LDA, notified the National Data Protection Commission (CNPD) of a processing of personal data in order to organize a computerized record of activities related to the provision of services to customers.
Compliance with a legal obligation constitutes a basis of legitimacy for the treatment, under the terms of subparagraph b) of article 6 of Law no. August - Data Protection Law (LPD). Indeed, subparagraph c) of paragraph 1 of article 37 of Law no. 34/2013, of 16 May, imposes the obligation on private security companies to carry out this registration with the CNPD.
The information processed is collected in a lawful manner (cf. Article 5, no. 1, subparagraph a) of the LPD), for specific, explicit and legitimate purposes (cf. subparagraph b)) and the data to be processed are considered adequate, relevant and not excessive (cf. subparagraph c)), in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of article 38 of Law no. 34/2013.
The data must be accurate and, if necessary, updated (cf. Article 5, point d) of the LPD), being kept only for the period necessary to pursue the purposes of collection and further processing, and it is up to the person responsible take appropriate measures to ensure that inaccurate or incomplete data are erased or rectified (cf. Article 5(1)(c) and d), and(3) of the DPL).
At the time of data collection, the information provided for in article 10 of the LPD must be provided, as well as the right of access and rectification in accordance with article 11 of the LPD.
The data are not communicated to third parties, without prejudice to consultation by supervisory entities and other transmissions legally provided to the competent authorities.
The entity responsible for the treatment must adopt the appropriate security measures to guarantee the effective security of the information and personal data processed.
Thus, pursuant to the combined provisions of article 6(b) and article 30(1) of the LPD, the aforementioned data processing is registered in the following terms:
